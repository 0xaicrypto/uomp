---

**2/9**  
Enter UOMP — User-Owned Memory Protocol.  
A new open protocol draft that keeps your personal memory on your device and lets you grant Agents temporary, scoped, auditable access.  
Site: https://www.uomp.org

---

**3/9**  
How it works:
• Agent declares what it needs in a `uom.json` manifest.
• You review and authorize via a local CLI/GUI.
• It gets a short-lived Capability Token — scoped by tag or key.
• When the session ends, access dies with it.

---

**4/9**  
The result?
• Your data never moves to the Agent provider.
• You can revoke access instantly.
• Every read is logged — you know exactly what was seen.
• You can switch Agents without rebuilding your memory.

---

**5/9**  
Why does this matter for @Starknet?
Starknet is building verifiable, decentralized AI Agents.
UOMP can be the off-chain user-memory layer while Starknet handles identity, registry, session anchors, and proofs.

---

**6/9**  
Starknet primitives that fit perfectly:
• Account Abstraction → user identity & authorization.
• ERC-8004-style Registry → discover trusted Agents.
• Cheap on-chain storage → anchor session roots & revocations.
• Cairo → prove access compliance without revealing data.
